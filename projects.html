Interfaith Approaches to Covid Relief

What will it take for our Charlotte community to rise out of the pandemic crisis more deeply committed to the wellbeing of all? Five campus teams addressed this question, collaborating with partner organizations to build a sustainable model of interfaith cooperation.

Central Piedmont Community College

The CPCC team worked with the College's longtime partner, Loaves and Fishes, to offer halal and vegetarian food boxes for Muslim and Hindu families.

Learn more

Johnson C. Smith University

JCSU's team partnered with MECKMIN to produce a documentary series called "Crisis and Compassion" that highlights our community's religously-motivated care for those impacted by the pandemic.

Learn more

University of North Carolina at Charlotte & Wingate University

Teams from Wingate and UNC Charlotte created and distributed over 100 interfaith activity boxes through three non-profits addressing Covid-related learning loss: Freedom School Partners, Wayfinders, and Refugee Support Services.

Learn more

Queens University

The Queens team partnered with ourBridge for KIDS to strengthen their support for immigrant and refugee children made more vulnerable by the pandemic.

Learn more

Phase Two: Faith in the Vaccine Ambassadors

Fifty student ambassadors. Twelve community partners. Thousands of people reached. Trained with peers from across the country on the vaccine, community organizing, and storytelling, our student ambassadors and community partners are working together to address vaccine hesitancy with thoughtful, informed, and respectful outreach efforts.
